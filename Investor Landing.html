The problem

Existing apps log what you did. They don't tell you what to do next.

We analyzed thousands of real user voices across Reddit, X, app reviews and open-source repos. The pattern is consistent across personas, and it's not lack of discipline.

01

Cognitive overload of sequencing

Users repeatedly describe tools as "trackers, not planners." They still do the hardest mental work: figuring out the order, the time estimates, the priorities — every single day.

02

Plans collapse on contact with reality

A meeting runs long, energy crashes, the kid needs attention. The app doesn't adapt. The whole plan goes in the bin and overwhelm wins.

03

Shame from rigid streak systems

Streak mechanics punish normal life — illness, travel, low-energy days. The result is dropout, not discipline. Burnout-aware design is explicitly requested in every community.

04

Fragmentation and starting friction

Habits in one app, tasks in another, calendar elsewhere. ADHD users describe extreme paralysis when goals aren't broken into micro-sequences with realistic estimates.

The solution

An intelligent sequence planner that adapts to your real life and brain — not the other way around.

Core intelligence

Goals become micro-sequences automatically.

Speak or type a goal with your constraints — time, energy, deadline. A hybrid priority-queue + LLM pipeline produces 5–7 ordered micro-steps with realistic time estimates, plus a one-line rationale for each. Under 5 seconds, every time.

Input → output
"Get my portfolio site live before the meetup — 2 hours, medium energy"
2mOpen the project folder · tiny first step
10mList 5 sections missing copy · visible scope
25mDraft hero copy only · hardest while fresh
5mStand up + water · protects focus
30mWire one case study · concrete win
Real-time adaptation

"Life happened?" One button reshapes the day.

The defining UX moment. Tap one button and pick a shape: delay everything, simplify, swap a task, or generate a gentle recovery plan. The constraint solver pushes blocks forward, drops the lowest-score optional one, and shows you the diff. No shame. No streak penalty.

Non-punitive feedback

An effort bloom that only grows.

We replaced streaks. Cadence shows an effort bloom that grows with consistency and doesn't reset on a missed day. Insights surface patterns — "you finish 82% of sequences started before 10am" — never failure rates or lost streaks. Quiet by default, deep when you want it.

Transparent intelligence

"Why this order?" — never a black box.

Every step is scored on five sub-signals (urgency, importance, energy match, success likelihood, low effort) and a composite. Tap any block to see the breakdown. One button gives you a plain-language explanation of the whole day. Trust comes from legibility.
